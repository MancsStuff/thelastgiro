CRM & Tech Setup

The real setup guide — built from actually doing it. Every trap, every mistake, the correct order, and the free Zoho tools that replace expensive paid apps. This is the module nobody else will give you.

📖 Real Story — Why This Module Was Rewritten

This module was originally written as theory. Then the course creator actually launched The Last Giro and hit every single trap in it — the wrong Zoho account setup, Gumroad URLs changing, DNS records breaking, webhook links getting lost, Calendly vs Zoho Bookings confusion, Tidio vs SalesIQ confusion.

Every bit of wasted time in that launch has been turned into a warning in this module. You are reading the version that cost hours to learn. Follow it exactly and you'll set up in a fraction of the time.

The Zoho Suite — Your Free Business Stack

Before we go through setup — here's the most important thing to know. Zoho offers a complete free business toolkit that replaces every expensive paid tool you'll see advertised to small businesses. You don't need any of the paid alternatives.

Tidio (live chat)→Zoho SalesIQ — free
Calendly (scheduling)→Zoho Bookings — free
Zapier (automation)→Zoho Flow — free
Mailchimp (email marketing)→Zoho Campaigns — free
Salesforce/HubSpot (CRM)→Zoho CRM — free tier
Intercom (customer chat)→Zoho SalesIQ — free
✓ The Golden Rule

If someone tries to sell you a tool for your business — search "Zoho [tool name]" first. Zoho almost certainly has a free version. Stay in the Zoho ecosystem and your entire business stack costs nothing.

Critical Rule — One Zoho Account Per Business

⚠ Most Expensive Mistake in This Module

If you already have a Zoho account for another business — do NOT add your new business domain to it. Zoho's paid plans limit the number of domains. Adding a second business to an existing paid account will force you to upgrade.

Create a completely separate free Zoho account for each new business. Takes 5 minutes. Costs nothing. Saves hours of frustration.

This mistake was made during the launch of The Last Giro itself. Learn from it.

The Correct Setup Order

Do these in exactly this order. Setting them up in the wrong sequence causes problems — especially with Gumroad and the automation in Step 6.

Step 1 — Email (Zoho Mail — Free)

  • Go to zoho.com/mail — sign up for the FREE plan (not the paid workplace plan)
  • Create a brand new account — do not add to an existing Zoho account
  • Add your domain — type just the domain name e.g. yourbusiness.com (the www. prefix in the box is fine, Zoho strips it)
  • Verify domain ownership — Zoho will give you a TXT record to add in Porkbun or your DNS provider
  • Add the MX records Zoho gives you to your DNS — this routes email to Zoho
  • Create your email address: hello@yourbusiness.com
  • Free plan: 1 mailbox, 1 domain — that is all you need
If Zoho asks for an organisation name

Just put your business name. It is a label only — it does not create a legal entity or register anything.

Step 2 — Payments (PayPal + Stripe + Gumroad)

  • PayPal Business — paypal.com/business — free, send invoices, take card payments
  • Stripe — stripe.com — professional card payments, create payment links without coding. When asked for statement descriptor: use your business name in CAPITALS
  • Gumroad — gumroad.com — for selling digital products. Free to set up, takes ~10% per sale
⚠ Gumroad — Critical Trap

When you set up your Gumroad account — note your exact Gumroad username. Your product URLs will be: yourusername.gumroad.com/l/productname

During The Last Giro launch the Gumroad username changed from the original setup — every link on the website was broken and showing 404 errors. All buy buttons stopped working.

Before building your website: publish a test product on Gumroad, copy the exact URL, confirm it works. Then build your website with that URL. Not before.

Also: set up Gumroad and get your product URLs before you build your website. Building the site first means doing the work twice.

Step 3 — Client Tracking (Google Sheets — Free)

  • Open Google Sheets — create a new sheet called "Clients [Year]"
  • Columns: Name, Contact, What they need, Last contact date, Status, Monthly value
  • Review every Monday — if you haven't contacted a lead in 2 weeks, do it today
  • This is your CRM. Upgrade to Zoho CRM free tier when you outgrow it.

Step 4 — Communication (WhatsApp Business — Free)

  • Download WhatsApp Business — separate from personal WhatsApp
  • Set up business profile: name, description, hours, website
  • Add your services to the Catalogue section
  • Set auto-reply: "Thanks for getting in touch — I'll respond within a few hours."

Step 5 — Live Chat on Website (Zoho SalesIQ — Free)

Replaces Tidio. Does everything Tidio does. Free with your Zoho account.

  • Go to salesiq.zoho.com — sign in with your Zoho account
  • Create a portal — name it your business name
  • Go to Settings → Brands → customise widget colour to match your site
  • Go to Installation → copy the embed code
  • Paste the embed code into your website HTML just before the </body> tag
  • Download Zoho SalesIQ app on your phone for instant chat notifications
✓ Auto-response to set up in SalesIQ

Trigger: visitor lands on site → send: "Hi! Welcome. Module 1 is completely free — no email needed. Any questions just ask 👇"

Step 6 — Scheduling (Zoho Bookings — Free)

Replaces Calendly. Free with your Zoho account. Already better than Calendly for most small businesses.

  • Go to bookings.zoho.com — sign in with your Zoho account
  • Set up your profile and connect your Google Calendar or Zoho Calendar
  • Create Service 1: Free 15-Minute Chat — 15 mins, price: Free
  • Create Service 2 (if offering premium access): 30-min paid session
  • Set your availability
  • Copy your booking page URL — add it to your website and social bios
  • Zoho Bookings generates an embed code — add the booking button to your website
Zoho Bookings embed — what to expect

Zoho gives you a script tag to add to your website. It creates a floating "Book now" button. Paste it just before the </body> tag in your HTML — same place as the SalesIQ code. Both will work together.

Step 7 — Email Marketing (Zoho Campaigns — Free)

  • Go to campaigns.zoho.com — sign in with your Zoho account
  • Create your mailing list
  • Set up your welcome email automation sequence
  • Important: Zoho Campaigns requires account verification before automation goes live — can take up to 24 hours. Do this first, then set up the rest while you wait.
  • Your signup form URL goes in your TikTok bio and website
⚠ Zoho Campaigns approval wait

Zoho verifies new Campaigns accounts before allowing automated sequences. This takes up to 24 hours. Set it up on Day 1 — then do everything else while you wait. Don't leave it until last or you'll be stuck waiting before you can launch your email sequence.

Step 8 — Automation (Zoho Flow — Free)

Replaces Zapier. Free with your Zoho account. Connects Gumroad and Stripe to Zoho Campaigns automatically.

⚠ The Webhook Trap — Read This Carefully

When setting up Zoho Flow to connect with Gumroad — Zoho Flow gives you a webhook URL. You paste this into Gumroad's Advanced Settings → Ping section.

The trap: In Gumroad Advanced Settings there is also a "Create application" section that looks similar. Do NOT use that. Scroll past it to find the separate Ping section at the bottom.

If you accidentally click into the application creator — go back to gumroad.com/settings/advanced and scroll to the very bottom to find the real Ping box.

Save the webhook URL somewhere before you paste it — in your Google Drive or Notes app. If you lose it you have to go back into Zoho Flow to find it again.

Automation 1 — Gumroad buyer → Zoho Campaigns:

  • Go to flow.zoho.com → Create Flow
  • Trigger: Gumroad — New Sale
  • Action: Zoho Campaigns — Add Subscriber to your list
  • Action 2: Zoho Campaigns — Start your welcome email sequence
  • Copy the webhook URL Zoho Flow gives you
  • Go to gumroad.com/settings/advanced → scroll to Ping section → paste URL → click Update Settings
  • Turn the flow ON and test with a real purchase

Automation 2 — Stripe sale → email notification to you:

  • Create new Flow → Trigger: Stripe — New Payment
  • Action: Zoho Mail → Send Email to hello@yourbusiness.com
  • Body: "New Stripe sale from [customer email]. Generate their access code at yourdomain.com/admin.html"
  • Turn ON

Step 9 — File Storage (Google Drive — Free)

  • Create a folder per client in Google Drive
  • Sub-folder: "Business Expenses [Year]" — screenshot every receipt here immediately
  • Share files with clients via Drive link — never email attachments
