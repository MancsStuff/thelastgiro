09

CRM & Tech Setup

The complete account setup guide in the correct order — with warnings about every trap that wastes time and money. Do this once, do it right.

⚠ Set Up in This Order — Do Not Skip Steps

Setting these up in the wrong order causes problems. Do them exactly in the sequence below. Some need a laptop — marked clearly.

Step 1 — Email (Phone or Laptop)

Use Zoho Mail free plan — gives you a professional email like hello@yourbusiness.com completely free.

  • Go to zoho.com/mail and sign up for the FREE plan
  • Critical rule: set up a SEPARATE free Zoho account for each business. Never try to add a second business domain to an existing Zoho paid account — you will hit walls and potentially pay for upgrades you don't need.
  • Add your domain, verify it, create your email address
  • The free plan allows 1 user and 1 domain — that's all you need to start
Zoho free plan limit — what to expect

The free plan only allows 1 mailbox. If you see a message about needing a licence when adding users — you've hit this limit. Solution: you only need 1 mailbox. Don't upgrade.

If Zoho asks for an organisation name — just put your business name. It's a label only, doesn't create a legal entity.

Step 2 — Payments (Phone or Laptop)

  • PayPal Business — free to set up, small % per transaction, send invoices directly. paypal.com/business
  • Stripe — professional card payments, create payment links without coding. stripe.com — when asked for statement descriptor, use your business name in capitals e.g. THELASTGIRO
  • Gumroad — for selling digital products (courses, PDFs, guides). gumroad.com — free to set up, takes ~10% per sale (this is a deductible expense)
⚠ Gumroad setup order — critical

Set up Gumroad and create your products before you build your website. You need the Gumroad product links to put on your buy buttons. Building the site first means doing the work twice.

Also: add your earnings disclaimer and T&Cs to every Gumroad product description before publishing. Not after.

Step 3 — Client Tracking (Phone or Laptop)

  • Google Sheets (free) — Name, contact, what they need, last contact date, status, monthly value. That's your CRM.
  • Review it every Monday. If you haven't contacted a lead in 2 weeks — do it today.

Step 4 — Communication (Phone)

  • WhatsApp Business (free) — separate from personal, business profile, services catalogue, auto-reply
  • Set up a quick reply: "Thanks for getting in touch — I'll respond within a few hours."
  • Add your services to the Catalogue section — clients can see what you offer before they message

Step 5 — File Storage (Phone or Laptop)

  • Google Drive (15GB free) — one folder per client, share files directly rather than emailing attachments
  • Create a folder: "Business Expenses [Year]" — sub-folders per month — screenshot every receipt immediately

Step 6 — Scheduling (Phone or Laptop)

  • Calendly free tier — let clients book calls automatically, connects to Google Calendar
  • Saves hours of back-and-forth every week

Step 7 — Email Marketing (Laptop recommended)

  • Mailchimp free tier — up to 500 subscribers free
  • Signup form is under Forms in the left menu — NOT Audience settings
  • Use the form URL directly in your website — not the embed code
AI Prompt — Your Setup Checklist

"I'm setting up a [business type] as a sole trader on a tight budget. Give me step-by-step setup instructions for: (1) Zoho free email with my domain, (2) PayPal Business account, (3) WhatsApp Business profile with a service catalogue, (4) a simple monthly income and expenses tracker in Google Sheets. Free tools only. Mobile-first instructions."

✓ Before You Move to the Next Module — Confirm These
  • I have a professional email address set up — hello@mybusiness.com or similar
  • I have PayPal Business set up and can take payments today
  • I have WhatsApp Business set up with my services in the catalogue
  • I have a Google Sheet tracking my income and expenses
  • I have set up Gumroad (if selling digital products) BEFORE building my website
