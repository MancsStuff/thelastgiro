Tax, DWP & Keeping More Money

The module that changes everything. Most people on benefits leave hundreds of pounds a month on the table because they don't know what they can legally deduct. This is the honest, practical guide — based on real experience.

The Golden Rule — Memorise This

You report PROFIT — not income — to both HMRC and the DWP.

Profit = Money received MINUS allowable business expenses.

Every legitimate expense you claim reduces your taxable income AND reduces the earnings DWP uses to calculate your UC. Done properly, expenses don't just save tax — they directly increase your Universal Credit every month.

UC Taper — The Real Maths

DWP takes 55p of every £1 of profit over your work allowance. The taper applies to PROFIT — not income. Expenses make the difference.

Worked example — real numbers

Gumroad course sales income: £320.00

Less Gumroad fees (10%): -£32.00

Less Super Whisper subscription: -£8.00

Less phone bill (50% business): -£12.50

Less home office flat rate (26hrs): -£10.00

Less this course purchase (training): -£9.99

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Profit to report to DWP: £247.51 — not £320

That £72.49 difference in expenses saves you £39.87 in UC (at 55% taper). Every month. Forever.

Every Allowable Expense

Technology & Equipment

  • Your smartphone — the business-use proportion (e.g. 50% if used half for work)
  • Laptop or tablet — business-use proportion
  • Headphones, ring light, tripod, phone stand — if bought for business

Software & Subscriptions

  • Super Whisper (~£8/month) — fully deductible
  • Adobe Express paid tier — fully deductible
  • Any paid AI tool subscription used for business
  • Gumroad or Payhip fees — cost of sale, fully deductible
  • Website hosting, domain costs
  • This course (The Last Giro £9.99) — yes, it is a deductible business expense

Home Office — Use of Home

HMRC simplified flat rate — no receipts needed, just record your hours:

  • 25–50 hours business use per month = £10/month
  • 51–100 hours per month = £18/month
  • 101+ hours per month = £26/month

Phone & Broadband

  • Home broadband — claim the business-use proportion (e.g. 40%)
  • Mobile phone contract — business-use proportion

What You Cannot Claim

  • Personal food and drink
  • Clothing (unless a uniform)
  • Personal entertainment, gym, holidays
  • The full cost of something used partly personally — business proportion only

DWP Monthly Reporting — Exact Steps

Do this on the 26th of every month. Set a phone alarm now.

  • Step 1 — Add up all income received in your assessment period (27th–26th)
  • Step 2 — Add up all allowable expenses from your expenses spreadsheet
  • Step 3 — Subtract: income minus expenses = profit
  • Step 4 — Log into your UC online account
  • Step 5 — Go to your journal, add a note or report earnings as prompted
  • Step 6 — Report the PROFIT figure — not the income figure
  • Step 7 — Screenshot the confirmation page and save to Google Drive
Exact wording to use in your UC journal

"Self-employed income this assessment period: £[income]. Allowable business expenses: £[expenses]. Net profit to declare: £[profit]. Business name: [your business name]. Nature of business: [what you do]."

⚠ Minimum Income Floor — Know This Before Month 12

After 12 months of self-employment, DWP may apply the Minimum Income Floor — an assumed earnings level based on National Living Wage. If your actual profit is below this, they calculate your UC as if you earned the MIF amount. Does NOT apply in your first 12 months. May not apply if you have a health condition. Ask your work coach before month 12.

Self Assessment — Annual Tax Return

  • 5 April — end of tax year
  • 31 January — online filing deadline
  • No tax on first £12,570 profit (Personal Allowance)
  • 20% Income Tax on profit between £12,571 and £50,270
  • Class 4 NI: 9% on profit above £12,570
  • If your annual profit is under £12,570 — you pay zero Income Tax
